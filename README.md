## 1. 🔍 The 3‑Step Extraction Process

| Step                            | What to do                                               | Example from resume                   |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **1. Spot achievements**        | Find numbers, percentages, dollar amounts, or time saved | “Increased sales by 30% in 6 months”  |
| **2. Identify hard skills**     | Look for tools, technologies, methodologies              | “Python, Tableau, Agile, Salesforce”  |
| **3. Extract role & seniority** | Pull your exact job title and level indicator            | “Senior Marketing Manager” → “Senior” |

**Resulting headline:** `Senior Marketing Manager | Salesforce, Agile | Grew sales 30%`

---

## 2. 📋 What to Copy from Your Resume (and What to Skip)

| Copy this ✅                             | Skip this ❌                                 |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Quantifiable results (“$500K saved”)    | Soft skills (“team player”)                 |
| Specific tools/languages (“AWS, React”) | Overused buzzwords (“synergy,” “paradigm”)  |
| Leadership indicators (“Led team of 8”) | Duties without outcomes (“responsible for”) |
| Certifications (“PMP, CFA Level 2”)     | Long company descriptions                   |
| Your actual job title                   | Internal jargon no one knows                |

**💡 Pro tip:** If you can’t find numbers in your resume, go back to your projects or performance reviews. Even “trained 3 new hires” is a number.

---

## 3. 📊 How to Quantify When You Have Zero Metrics

No hard numbers? Use these alternatives:

| Alternative proof | Example headline phrase                           |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Scale / scope     | “Managed $50K budget” or “Supported 200+ clients” |
| Time frame        | “Completed 2‑month project in 3 weeks”            |
| Frequency         | “Published weekly reports for 1 year”             |
| Efficiency gain   | “Automated manual process saving 10 hours/week”   |
| Recognition       | “Received ‘Rising Star’ award”                    |

**Or use action verbs alone:** “Launched,” “Rebuilt,” “Designed,” “Negotiated,” “Streamlined.”

---

## 4. ⚠️ Common Resume‑to‑Headline Mistakes

| Mistake                     | Why it fails                           | Fix                                                          |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Copying a full bullet point | Too long for 220 characters            | Extract only the number + skill: “Cut costs 15%” instead of “Implemented new procurement process that cut costs by 15% through vendor renegotiation” |
| Using past tense everywhere | Headlines read better in present tense | Change “Improved” to “Improve” or use noun form: “Cost reduction specialist” |
| Listing 8 skills            | Looks like keyword stuffing            | Pick 2–4 most relevant for your target role                  |
| Forgetting your target role | Recruiters don’t know what you want    | Add “Seeking [role]” or imply it in the role                 |

## 6. ✍️ Quick Worksheet: Extract Your Own Headline

Take your most impressive resume bullet and fill this out:

1. **Job title / role:** _______________  
2. **One number or metric:** _______________  
3. **Top 2 skills from that bullet:** _______________  
4. **Action verb (past → present):** _______________  

**Combine:** `[Role] | [Skills] | [Present‑tense action + metric]`

**Example:**  
`Data Analyst | SQL, Tableau | Build dashboards serving 50+ users`
